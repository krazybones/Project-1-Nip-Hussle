The Man

Ermias Joseph Asghedom, professionally known as Nipsey Hussle/Nipsey Hu$$le, was an American rapper, activist, entrepreneur, father, and leader.

Born on August 15, 1985 in Crenshaw, Los Angeles, California to an African American woman and Eritrean immigrant father. At the age of 14, Nipsey left home and joined the local Rollin 60’s Neighborhood Crips.

On March 31, 2019, Hussle was shot at least 10 times, by a man named Eric Holder, in the parking lot of his Marathon Clothing store, in South Los Angeles. His death was attributed to gunshot wounds to his right chest, abdomen, lung, and scalp. He was 33 years old.

The Music

Hussle emerged on the hip-hop scene in the mid-200s, independently releasing his first mixtape, Slauson Boy Volume 1. He created his record label, All Money In, after completing a brief stint at Epic Records.

Hussle’s discography includes 1 studio album, 3 compilation mixtapes, 13 mixtapes, and 49 singles (including 19 as a featured artist).

The World After

Also well known for his community activism, Hussle denounced gun violence through his music and community work. He also spoke openly about his experiences with gang culture.

He funded improvements to neighborhood schools and spent time with students as well as participating on panels about growing up in the area and the influence of gang culture. He also started a co-working environment which he named Vector 90, which provided an avenue for youths to take classes in science, technology, and mathematics.
